LOS SANTOS CRIMINAL CODE

The justice system works as follows: If you are caught committing a crime or driving violation you have the option to pay the fine issued and serve the sentence issued right away. The sentence will be decided by the arresting officer based on the Criminal Code, situation and the offenders previous record. The accused has the option to fight the case via a bench trial providing a judge is available. The judge will review the evidience and statements from the arresting officer and the suspect and determine which charges, if any, will be pressed. This applies for criminal and driving offences. The accused can also decide to wait for a judge to be available and go to trial. If you chose this route, you will be held until trial. The trial will be a bench trial for everything except serious crimes. In the case of serious crimes, the trial will be a jury trial. The third option is to pay the fine, serve the time, and plead "not guilty." After serving the sentence, contest the charges by fileing in court within three days of the sentencing. The trial will be a bench trial for everything except serious crimes. In the case of serious crimes, the trial wil be a jury trial. If you win the case, you will be refunded the money you paid in fines. There is no compensation for time served. You are free to hire a lawyer at any point.

FELONIES: The most serious type of criminal charge. Carry a penalty of more than a year in prison, as well as fines, probation or parole, and other penalties.

PENAL CODE FELONIES (1) - Murder (1.1) to (1.6)

(1.1) First Degree Murder

Fine: $20,000
Maximum Sentence: 60 Months
Any intentional murder that is willful and premeditated with malice aforethought. Eligible for the death penalty.

(1.2) Second Degree Murder

Fine: $15,000
Maximum Sentence: 45 Months
Any intentional murder with malice aforethought, but is not premeditated or planned in advance

(1.3) Murder of a Government Official

Fine: $30,000
Maximum Sentence: 100 Months
Any intentional murder that is willful and premeditated with malice aforethought against a Government Official (EMT, LEO, Judge, Ect.) Eligible for the death penalty.

(1.4) DUI Murder

Fine: $12,000
Maximum Sentence: 30 Months
Involuntary Manslaughter that occurs while the perpetrator is under the influence

(1.5) Attempted Murder

Fine: $10,000
Maximum Sentence: 30 Months
Attempted murder is an intent to murder, meaning that the perpetrator either tried to murder and failed or took a substantial step towards committing a murder

(1.6) Attempted Murder of a Government Official

Fine: $15,000
Maximum Sentence: 50 Months
Attempted murder of a Government Official is an intent to murder, meaning that the perpetrator either tried to murder and failed or took a substantial step towards committing a murder of a Government Official (EMT, LEO, Judge, Ect.)

PENAL CODE FELONIES (2) - Violent Crimes (2.1) to (2.15)

(2.1) Voluntary Manslaughter

Fine: $15,000
Maximum Sentence: 30 Months
Sometimes called a crime of passion murder, Voluntary Manslaughter is any intentional killing that involves no prior intent to kill, and which was committed under such circumstances that would "cause a reasonable person to become emotionally or mentally disturbed".

(2.2) Involuntary Manslaughter

Fine: $8,000
Maximum Sentence: 20 Months
A killing that stems from a lack of intention to cause death but involving an intentional or negligent act leading to death

(2.3) Vehicular Manslaughter

Fine: $10,000
Maximum Sentence: 25 Months
A killing with the use of a vehicle that stems from a lack of intention to cause death but involving an intentional or negligent act leading to death

(2.4) Torture

Fine: $8,000
Maximum Sentence: 20 Months
The action or practice of inflicting severe pain on someone as a punishment or to force them to do or say something, or for the pleasure of the person inflicting the pain

(2.5) False Imprisonment

Fine: $2,500
Maximum Sentence: 10 Months
False imprisonment occurs when a person is restricted in their personal movement within any area without justification or consent. Actual physical restraint is not necessary for false imprisonment to occur. A false imprisonment claim may be made based upon private acts, or upon wrongful governmental detention

((2.6) Assault with a Deadly Weapon

Fine: $5,000
Maximum Sentence: 12 Months
An assault with a deadly weapon occurs when an attacker accompanies a physical attack with a physical object capable of inflicting serious bodily injury or death, by virtue of its design or construction

(2.7) Battery on a Peace Officer

Fine: $10,000
Maximum Sentence: 25 Months
Battery is defined as any willful and unlawful use of force or violence upon the person of another. If you knowingly use unlawful force against a peace officer while he or she is performing their duties you have commited Battery on a Peace Officer

(2.8) Bank Robbery

Fine: $15,000
Maximum Sentence: 20 Months
The act of stealing from a bank while employees and bystanders are subjected to violence, the threat of violence, or force

(2.9) Aggravated Armed Robbery

Fine: $20,000
Maximum Sentence: 30 Months
\ Aggravated robbery means the robber was armed with a deadly weapon, had an accomplice, or actually inflicted serious bodily harm on the victim

(2.10) Armed Robbery

Fine: $7,500
Maximum Sentence: 20 Months
A person commits armed robbery when he takes something from someone else, using violence or intimidation, while carrying a dangerous weapon.

(2.11) Kidnapping

Fine: $4,000
Maximum Sentence: 15 Months
Kidnapping is the unlawful carrying away and confinement of a person against their will

(2.12) Arson

Fine: $1,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Arson is the crime of willfully and maliciously setting fire to or charring property.

(2.13) Aggravated Arson

Fine: $3,000
Maximum Sentence: 15 Months
Aggravated Arson is an arson accompanied by some aggravating factor, as when the offender foresees or anticipates that one or more persons will be in or near the property being burned.

(2.14) Terroristic Acts

Fine: $30,000
Maximum Sentence: Mandatory 3 day hold
The unlawful use of force and violence against persons or property to intimidate or coerce a government, the civilian population, or any segment thereof, in furtherance of political or social objectives

(2.15) Terroristic Threat

Fine: $5,000
Maximum Sentence: 10 Months
A crime generally involving a threat to commit violence communicated with the intent to terrorize another, to cause evacuation of a building, or to cause serious public inconvenience, in reckless disregard of the risk of causing such terror or inconvenience

PENAL CODE FELONIES (3) - Firearms/Explosives (3.1) to (3.5)

(3.1) Possession of Explosives (Class 4)

Fine: $15,000
Maximum Sentence: Mandatory 3 day hold
Possession of an Explosive device (C4, RPG, Ect.)

(3.2) Possession of a Class 3 Firearm

Fine: $10,000
Maximum Sentence: 45 Months
Possession of a Class 3 Firearm (Assault rifle, sniper rifle, auto shotgun ect.)

(3.3) Possession of a Class 2 Firearm

Fine: $5,000
Maximum Sentence: 24 Months
Possession of a Class 2 Firearm (Autopistol, autobolter, bolter, needler or SMG)

(3.4) Criminal Possession of a Class 1 Firearm

Fine: $3,000
Maximum Sentence: 12 Months
Possession of a Class 1 Firearm (All legal weapons) without a valid license

(3.5) Unlawful Discharge of a Firearm

Fine: $5,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Willfully discharge a firearm in a grossly negligent manner which could result in someone's injury or death.

PENAL CODE FELONIES (4) - Narcotics (4.1) to (4.5)

(4.1) Drug Cultivation and Manufacturing

Fine: $8,000
Maximum Sentence: 25 Months
The act of planting, harvesting, or processing of a Class 2 or Class 1 Drug.

(4.2) Class 2 Drug Distribution/Trafficking

Fine: $6,000
Maximum Sentence: 20 Months
Possession of 5 or more units of a Class 2 Drug (Cocaine, Meth, Heroin)

(4.3) Class 1 Drug Distribution/Trafficking

Fine: $2,500
Maximum Sentence: 10 Months
Possession of 10 or more units of a Class 1 Drug (Marijuana)

(4.4) Possession of Class 2 Drugs

Fine: $2,500
Maximum Sentence: 10 Months
Possession of less than 5 units of a Class 2 Drug (Cocaine, Meth, Heroin)

(4.5) Possession of Class 1 Drugs

Fine: $1,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Possession of less than 10 units of a Class 1 Drug (Marijuana)

PENAL CODE FELONIES (5) - Corruption (5.1) to (5.4)

(5.1) Bribery

Fine: $1,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Bribery is the act of giving or receiving something of value in exchange for some kind of influence or action in return, that the recipient would otherwise not offer.

(5.2) Extortion

Fine: $3,000
Maximum Sentence: 15 Months
Extortion is a criminal offense of obtaining money, property, or services from an individual or institution, through coercion

(5.3) Money Laundering

Fine: $2,000
Maximum Sentence: 10 Months
Money laundering is the process of concealing the origins of money obtained illegally by passing it through a complex sequence of banking transfers or commercial transactions. The overall scheme of this process returns the money to the launderer in an obscure and indirect way

(5.4) Racketeering/RICO Act

Fine: $4,000
Maximum Sentence: 20 Months
Racketeering is an organized criminal act in which the criminal act is some form of substantial business, or a way to earn illegal money either regularly, or briefly but repeatedly. Racketeering is therefore generally a repeated or continuous criminal operation.

PENAL CODE FELONIES (6) - Vehicular Related Felonies (6.1) to (6.6)

(6.1) Grand Theft Auto

Fine: $2,000
Maximum Sentence: 12 Months
Grand theft auto is the act of stealing an unoccupied car or other vehicle.

(6.2) Carjacking

Fine: $3,000
Maximum Sentence: 16 Months
the action of violently stealing an occupied car.

(6.3) Violation of a No Fly Zone

Fine: $5,000
Maximum Sentence: 20 Months
Violation of a No Fly Zone is flying in a territory or an area over which aircraft are not permitted to fly.

(6.4) Felony Driving Under the Influence

Fine: $5,000
Maximum Sentence: 20 Months
Driving under the influence with an aggravating factor. (Causing bodily harm, Breaking other laws and driving with a suspended license, or prior convictions for DUI)

(6.5) Felony Evading Arrest

Fine: $3,00
Maximum Sentence: 15 Months
Intentionally fleeing from a person who they know is a police officer attempting to lawfully arrest or detain them in a motor vehicle and driving dangerously

(6.6) Felony Speeding (Driving over 100 MPH)

Fine: $1,500
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Travelling in a vehicle at speeds in excess of 100 MPH

PENAL CODE FELONIES (7) - Misc. Felonies (7.1) to (7.10)

(7.1) Obstruction of Justice

Fine: $3,500
Maximum Sentence: 18 Months
Obstruction of justice is a process crime, consisting of obstructing prosecutors or other (usually government) officials.

(7.2) Tampering with Evidence

Fine: $1,000
Maximum Sentence: 7 Months
Tampering with Evidence is an act in which a person alters, conceals, falsifies, or destroys evidence with the intent to interfere with an investigation (usually) by a law-enforcement, governmental, or regulatory authority.

(7.3) Burglary

Fine: $1,500
Maximum Sentence: 10 Months
Entry into a building illegally with intent to commit a crime, especially theft.

(7.4) Escaping Custody

Fine: $1,500
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
A person convicted of, charged with, or arrested for a misdemeanor, who intentionally escapes, or attempts to escape, from a detention facility, community-based correctional facility, or institution to which the person has been committed by reason of the conviction, charge, or arrest, or from the custody of any public officer, public employee, or any other person to whom the person has been entrusted

(7.5) Conspiracy

Fine: $10,000
Maximum Sentence: 25 Months
A conspiracy is a secret plan or agreement between persons (called conspirers or conspirators) for an unlawful or harmful purpose,

(7.6) False Impersonation of a Lawyer or Government Official

Fine: $10,000
Maximum Sentence: 25 Months
False Impersonation of a Lawyer or Government Official is falsely assuming or pretending to be an officer or employee acting under the authority of the United States or any department, agency or officer thereof, and acts as such, or in such pretended character demands or obtains any money, paper, document, or thing of value

(7.7) Perjury

Fine: $5,000
Maximum Sentence: 10 Months
The offense of willfully telling an untruth in a court after having taken an oath or affirmation.

(7.8) Fraud

Fine: $1,000
Maximum Sentence: 8 Months
Fraud is intentional deception to secure unfair or unlawful gain, or to deprive a victim of a legal right.

(7.9) Aiding and Abetting / Accessory

Fine: 50% of the Criminal Charge
Maximum Sentence:50% of the Criminal Charge
\ A crime was committed, The accused aided, counseled, commanded, induced or procured the person committing the crime; The accused acted with the intent to facilitate the crime; and The accused acted before the crime was completed

(7.10) Criminal Threats

Fine: $1,200
Maximum Sentence: 3 Months
Any person who willfully threatens to commit a crime which will result in death or great bodily injury to another person, with the specific intent that the statement, made verbally, in writing, or by means of an electronic communication device, is to be taken as a threat, even if there is no intent of actually carrying it out

MISDEMEANORS: Up to a year in jail and a $3,000 fine.

PENAL CODE MISDEMEANORS (8) - Vehicular Related Misdemeanors (8.1) to (8.5)

(8.1) Driving under the Influence / Driving While Intoxicated (DUI / DWI)

Fine: $2,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
DWI refers to driving while intoxicated of alcohol with a blood alcohol content (BAC) over the legal limit, while DUI is used when the driver is charged with being under the influence of alcohol or drugs

(8.2) Hit and Run

Fine: $1,500
Maximum Sentence: 3 Months
Hit and run is defined as leaving the scene of an accident where there is any type of injury to a person, whether the injured person is a pedestrian or an occupant of a vehicle.

(8.3) Evading Arrest

Fine: $2,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Intentionally fleeing from a person who they know is a police officer attempting to lawfully arrest or detain them

(8.4) Driving without proof of a License

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
Driving without proof of a driver's license

(8.5) Driving with a suspended License

Fine: 750
Maximum Sentence: 2 Months
Driving with a suspended or revoked license

PENAL CODE MISDEMEANORS (9)- Misc. Misdemeanors (9.1) to (9.27)

(9.1) Assault

Fine: $5,000
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
An assault is the act of inflicting physical harm or unwanted physical contact upon a person or, in some specific legal definitions, a threat or attempt to commit such an action.

(9.2) Battery

Fine: $1,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Battery is a criminal offense involving the unlawful physical acting upon a threat, distinct from assault which is the act of creating apprehension of such contact.

(9.3) Fleeing and Eluding

Fine: $2,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Fleeing and eluding is a criminal offense that involves a failure to comply with the order of a law enforcement officer, specifically in the operation of a motor vehicle (automobile or water vessel) in such a way as to evade or attempt to evade law enforcement after being given a signal to stop.

(9.4) Disturbing the Peace

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
Disturbing the peace, also known as breach of the peace, is a criminal offense that occurs when a person engages in some form of unruly public behavior, such as fighting or causing excessively loud noise

(9.5) Attempted Grand Theft Auto

Fine: $1,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
Attempted Grand theft auto is the attempted act of stealing an unoccupied car or other vehicle.

(9.6) Failure to Inform

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
The intentional failure to inform law enforcement of a concealed weapon or weapons during a legal detainment

(9.7) Reckless Endangerment

Fine: $2,500
Maximum Sentence: 12 Months
A person commits the crime of reckless endangerment if the person recklessly engages in conduct which creates substantial jeopardy of severe corporeal trauma to another person

(9.8) Resisting Arrest

Fine: $1,500
Maximum Sentence: 10 Months
fleeing a police officer while being arrested. threatening a police officer with physical violence while being arrested

(9.9) Unlawful Discharge of a Firearm

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
firing any weapon in certain areas or under specific circumstances, such as firing from a moving vehicle, firing across a state highway, or firing into or at an occupied building

(9.10) Brandishing a Firearm

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
that you drew or exhibited a deadly weapon or firearm in the presence of another person, and that you either 1. Did so in a rude, angry, or threatening manner or 2 you did so unlawfully in a fight or quarrel, and that you were not acting in self-defense or in the defense of another person at the time

(9.11) Failure to Identify

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
A person commits an offense if he intentionally refuses to give his name, residence address, or date of birth to a peace officer who has lawfully arrested the person and requested the information

(9.12) False Report

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
False Report is involving a person who, with intent to deceive, knowingly makes a false statement that is material to the criminal investigation of a crime and makes the statement to a peace officer or law enforcement official

(9.13) Failure to comply with a lawful order

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
A person may not wilfully fail or refuse to comply with a lawful order or direction of a police officer

(9.14) Possession of Burglary Tools

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
Possession of Burglary tools including but not limited to lockpicks.

(9.15) Breaking and Entering

Fine: $1,000
Maximum Sentence: 5 Months
the crime of entering a building by force so as to commit burglary.

(9.16) Stalking

Maximum Sentence: 3 Months
Stalking is a pattern of behavior that makes you feel afraid, nervous, harassed, or in danger. It is when someone repeatedly contacts you, follows you, sends you messages

(9.17) Jaywalking

(9.18) Loitering

Fine: $300
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months

(9.19) Receiving Stolen Property

Fine: $300
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months

(9.20) Reckless Driving

Reckless driving is often defined as a mental state in which the driver displays a wanton disregard for the rules of the road ckle

(9.21) Public Intoxication

Being drunk in public

Contempt of court, often referred to simply as "contempt", is the offense of being disobedient to or disrespectful toward a court of law and its officers in the form of behavior that opposes or defies the authority, justice and dignity of the court

(9.25) Trespassing

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
enter the owner's land or property without permission

(9.26) Petty Theft

Fine: $500
Maximum Sentence: 1 Months
Petty theft is a type of theft crime that is based on the low value of the stolen property. Under 400 dollars.

(9.27) Mental Evaluation

Fine: No Fine Maximum Sentence: Mandatory 3 day hold Mental Evaluation is defined as a way of assessing an individual's behavior, personality, cognitive abilities, and several other domains

INFRACTIONS: Carry a fine and no jail time or other restrictions. Minor traffic tickets are the most common example.
